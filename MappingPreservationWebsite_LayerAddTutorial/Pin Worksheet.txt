Images


1. Naming your Images
   1. Here is how to name your article images: “projectkeyword_image1”
      1. All images will have the same “projectkeyword” i.e. “Hollyhock.” You should then name your images “image1,2,3,4,etc.” in the order that you wish for them to appear, “image1” appearing first. Image titles should contain NO CAPITALS and NO SPACES. 


Worksheet Instructions


2. Start by copying this worksheet and renaming it before you edit the text. 
   1. Open this Worksheet file in Google Drive: 
      1. Go to “File>Make a Copy” in the upper left of Google Sheets. 
      2. In the dialog window that appears, change the name to  “Your Name_phase1”. 
      3. Make sure it is in the “Worksheets” folder, that “share it with the same people” is checked, and click ok. This will create a unique document for you to work from.


3. Edit Worksheet / “Markdown” file

Notes on Worksheet Elements
* Categories
   * Select 1-3 of the following categories to apply to your Pin: 
      * TBD collectively as a class


* Author
   * This will be your name as you wish it to appear below the title in your Post. If you would like to keep the post anonymous (i.e. not associated with your name/identity) please indicate so on the worksheet by writing “Anonymous Student” for “author.” 
* Bibliography
   * Your entries should be written following the Chicago Manual of Style. 
* Lat / Long*
   * This is how your pin will be geolocated on the site map. 
   * You can find the latitude and longitude of your site by: 
      * 1. Going to google maps and finding your site. 
      * 2. Click on the map where your pin is located. 
      * 3. Right click on the pin, then click on the lat, long that appears at the top of the pop-up that appears. This will automatically copy it to your clipboard. 
      * Paste into this doc, then move the first number (latitude) to “Lat” and second (longitude) to “Long” in the Worksheet.
