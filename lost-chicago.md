<h2>Lost Chicago: Demolished Sites and Collective Memory</h2>

<p>
  <em>Lost Chicago</em> is a spatial research project documenting 100 significant buildings and places across Chicago that have been demolished. The project maps sites of collective memory — amusement parks, hotels, industrial complexes, public housing, theaters, and civic institutions — charting what the city once held and what replaced it.
</p>

<p>
  Each site is documented with historical context, year of construction and demolition, cause of loss, and what now occupies the land. Together, the dataset reveals patterns of erasure across neighborhoods, building types, and eras of development.
</p>

<p>
  Toggle the <strong>Lost Chicago</strong> layer in the map legend to explore the sites spatially. Click any point to view historical and current photographs, description, and source documentation.
</p>
