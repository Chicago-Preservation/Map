#### Geography/Neighborhood

Margie’s Candies was founded in the Bucktown neighborhood of Logan Square, in the northwest of Chicago’s Loop. The northeast corner of Bucktown intersects with Milwaukee Avenue, one of Chicago's main thoroughfares. The first arrivals in Bucktown were Polish immigrants in 1830, soon followed by German immigrants (Bucktown Community Organization). Towards the beginning of World War I, the Bucktown area experienced an influx of Polish immigrants, and in the 1930s bringing in a new demographic of Russian-Jewish immigrants. By 1930, Logan Square was a predominantly White area, with the majority of the population composed of immigrants or their children (Encyclopedia of Chicago). 

The next shift in demographics came in the 1960s, with an influx of Hispanic people moving to the area, making up two-thirds of Logan Square's population by the 1990s. Present-day Bucktown/Logan Square has shifted to an upper-class neighborhood due to gentrification (Encyclopedia of Chicago).

#### The Family/Community

Margie’s Candies is a family owned business now spanning four generations. Greek immigrant Peter George Poulos was the founder of Margie’s Candies, called Security Sweet Shop, in its 1921 founding. Prior to immigrating to the United States, Poulos made candy for fishermen in the Greek town of Sparta (Weimer, 1999).  In 1933, Poulos’ son, George, took over the store with his wife, Margie –who also was a child of Greek immigrants– renaming the store to Margie’s Candies. As the store’s namesake, Margie was seen as the “rock” of the business and came up with the fudge recipe used by the shop (Weimer, 1999). 

Margie ran the store up until her passing in 1995. Her son, Peter, known as “Margie’s boy” to customers and employees, took ownership to run the store just as his mom did up until his death in 2023. Peter’s son, George, is the current owner of Margie’s (Bloom, 2023).

#### The Business/Commercial History

Since the shop’s founding in 1921, Margie’s has been serving candy and ice cream, alongside a small selection of savory food items. Hand-dipped chocolates, terrapins, as well as homemade ice cream creations –a “sweet assault” (Travis, 1976)– using the same techniques since the shop opened its doors has cemented Margie’s as a classic ice cream parlor that generations continually come back to. The atomic buster, a sundae that the Beatles indulged in after their 1965 concert at Comiskey Park (Chicago Tribune, 2004) are staple offerings at Margie’s that signify commitments to tradition. 

The physical character of Margie’s carries this ‘classic’ feel that echoes the same sense of nostalgia that the menu echoes. Mini juke-boxes, the neon ‘Margie’s Candies’ sign, and classic counter showcasing candies in the middle of the restaurant, maintain that ‘vintage’ look that transports and draws customers back to Margie’s for a “refreshing trip back to a simpler time” (Langer, 1987).
