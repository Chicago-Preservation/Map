#### History of the Neighborhood
Since Manny’s founding, there have been major changes to the infrastructure and demographics of the Near West Side. The surrounding blocks have been enlarged, while the nearby railyards have been reduced. Much of the surrounding neighborhood was demolished in the 60s, to make way for both the Dan Ryan Expressway and UIC’s campus expansions. The businesses around Manny’s have also changed, such as Chernin’s Shoestore, which was “part of the Manny’s ritual” and is no longer in business. The area has shifted from being mostly Eastern European immigrants, to being mostly black by the time Manny’s moved to its current location in 1964, and nowadays the area is very diverse. The total population, 66,000, is half of what it was at Manny’s founding, likely due to the expansion of UIC and the destruction of much of the surrounding neighborhoods.

#### History of the Family
Manny’s showcases its founder’s, Jack Raskin, Russian Jewish background with both the food it serves, and with where it’s located. Manny’s sells both Jewish deli and bakery goods, many of which are family recipes. Located in what used to be a historic Jewish neighborhood, Manny’s is also near to where the old Maxwell Street Market used to be. The market was known for its many Jewish businesses. Since its founding in 1942, Manny’s has been passed down from one generation of Raskins to the next, and is currently owned by Dan Raskin, the fourth-generation owner of the restaurant.

#### History of the Business
Since Manny’s opening in the 1940s, there have been multiple changes to both what the establishment offers and the building itself. Dan Raskin said that they have tried to modernize Manny’s “without changing the core of what they are.” While these “modernizations” have definitely increased business, “most regular customers aren't impressed by the politicians or the new fare. They just come for a good hearty cafeteria-style meal and some quality conversation.” Since moving to their current building in 1964, the look of Manny’s has stayed mostly the same. 
The biggest change was in 2015 when they renovated and expanded, bringing back the deli counter for the first time since their original location, as well as a bakery. Even as their customer base has changed, Manny’s stays true to their purpose of serving high quality, authentic Jewish food.
