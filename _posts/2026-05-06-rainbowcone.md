#### History of the Neighborhood
Rainbow Cone is located in the Beverly neighborhood on Chicago's South Side, a community that is known for its stability and strong local identity. Unlike many neighborhoods in Chicago that experienced significant population loss or industrial decline during the mid-20th century, Beverly remained largely residential and middle-class. The area became known for its Irish-American population, historic home, and active community organizations. Western Avenue, where Rainbow cone is located, has long served as a large commercial area connecting different parts of the South Side. While other commercial areas in Chicago declined due to suburbanization and disinvestment, Beverly's business district remained relatively stable. This sustainability allowed long-standing businesses like Rainbow Cone to continue business without relocating. The neighborhood's consistent population and community support played an important role in sustaining the business over generations.

#### History of the Family
Rainbow Cone was founded in 1926 by Joseph Sapp, and would become a multi-generational family business. The Sapp family maintained ownership through years of economic and social change, passing the business down while preserving its original identity. Family ownership has been a key part of Rainbow Cone's success. Instead of franchising early or making other changes to the business model, each generation focused on maintaining tradition while making gradual updates. This method shows a broader pattern among Chicago's legacy businesses, that family continuity helps preserve both cultural and food traditions. Today, the business is still operated by the Sapp family, highlighting the importance of generational knowledge and community ties in sustaining small businesses over a long period of time.

#### History of the Business
Rainbow Cone is most famous for its signature five-layer ice cream cone, which includes chocolate, strawberry, Palmer House (vanilla with cherries and walnuts), pistachio, and orange sherbet. Instead of being scooped, the ice cream is sliced and stacked, which creates a unique appearance and taste that sets it apart from other ice cream shops. Since its founding in 1926, Rainbow Cone has adapted to changes in the economy and society while still maintaining its traditions. During the late 20th and early 21st centuries, many small, family-owned food
businesses struggled to compete with chain restaurants. However, Rainbow Cone remained successful by sticking to its history, uniqueness, and strong community ties. Recently, the business has expanded beyond its original location to other locations and seasonal pop-ups, introducing the brand to new areas while still maintaining the original location to highlight its foundation. This balance between tradition and expansion has allowed Rainbow Cone to remain popular in a changing economy.
